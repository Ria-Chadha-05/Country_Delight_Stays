01 — Executive Summary

A trusted brand, extending its promise into a category of its own.

Country Delight Stays is not a travel, tourism or hospitality product. It is the first mover in Curated Nature Experiences — a new category built on the same trust Country Delight has already earned through milk, dairy, fruits and vegetables. The adjacent-industry data below explains why now; it does not define what this product is.

Category definition

Country Delight is not entering hospitality, travel or tourism. It is extending its existing promise of healthy, authentic living beyond food — helping urban households reconnect with nature, family and slower living through trusted, curated Nature Experiences. Accommodation is one enabler inside that offering, never the product itself.

01

Why this product exists

Domestic travel in India grew 17.5% year-on-year in 2024 to nearly 295 crore visits, and government data shows travellers moving toward shorter, more frequent trips rather than single annual holidays. Country Delight already sits inside the daily rhythm of urban Indian households through its milk and dairy delivery — it has permission to extend that trust into how those same families spend their weekends.

02

Why demand is shifting

Independent research firms size India's wellness-travel demand between roughly $29–35 billion in 2025 depending on methodology, with digital-detox escapes the fastest-growing sub-category at a projected 17.3% CAGR through 2031. The Union Budget 2025–26 backed this shift directly, allocating an estimated $2.2 billion toward AYUSH-based wellness travel under the "Heal in India" initiative and cutting GST on wellness services from 18% to 5% — real policy evidence that reconnection and slow living are becoming mainstream priorities, not niche ones.

03

What opportunity exists

The players closest to this white space — StayVista and SaffronStays — are villa-rental platforms, not curated-experience or trust brands, and StayVista's own FY25 filings show a business still building toward its first at-scale profitable year. Nobody occupies the specific position of a trusted, everyday consumer brand extending into curated nature experiences.

04

Key recommendation

Country Delight Stays should not launch as a hotel-booking or villa-rental product. It should launch as a curated ecosystem of trusted nature experiences — starting with day experiences that require no overnight commitment, and expanding toward immersive weekend and workcation formats as trust and supply mature.

Bottom line

Country Delight has an earned right to trust that Airbnb, MakeMyTrip and StayVista do not have with the same households. The opportunity is not to out-list the accommodation platforms — it is to out-trust them, by building a category they aren't built to compete in.

02 — Product Vision

Before the product, the philosophy.

Every strategic choice later in this report — taxonomy, positioning, discovery flow — traces back to these five statements.

Mission

To make trusted, authentic nature experiences as easy to access as a daily glass of milk.

Vision

A country where reconnecting with nature is a normal weekend habit, not a rare annual escape.

Problem Statement

Urban Indians want to disconnect from city life, but discovery is fragmented, trust is inconsistent, and existing platforms optimise for choice, not for confidence.

Opportunity Statement

A brand already trusted for what enters the home has a reasonable shot at extending that trust to how the family spends its weekends.

Core Philosophy

We are not selling accommodation. We are selling the feeling people are missing — reconnection with nature, family and themselves — and the experience is simply how they get there.

03 — Industry Evolution

Six stages that led here. Tap each to expand.

1. Traditional Tourism

⌄
Travel organised around fixed destinations — a city, a monument, a hill station — booked through agents and consumed as a checklist of sights rather than an experience of a place.

2. Destination Tourism

⌄
The rise of branded hotels and organised circuits (Swadesh Darshan and similar government schemes) turned destinations into products, with infrastructure and marketing built around named places rather than named feelings.

3. Experience Economy

⌄
Platforms like Airbnb Experiences and Thrillophilia shifted spend from lodging alone to activities — cooking classes, trails, workshops — sold as memories rather than nights booked.

4. Eco Tourism

⌄
Sustainability entered mainstream travel decisions. Statista's India outlook notes travellers are increasingly seeking sustainable and eco-friendly options, and government schemes like Swadesh Darshan 2.0 explicitly foreground eco-friendly infrastructure.

5. Curated Nature Experiences

⌄
Farm visits, fruit picking and rural immersion have become the fastest-growing segment tracked in this report's adjacent research — India's farm/nature-based travel segment is expanding from $206.8 million in 2024 toward $487.1 million by 2030 at a 15.4% CAGR (Grand View Research). This is the demand signal a dedicated nature-experience category can be built on.

6. Country Delight Enters

⌄
Not another entrant into travel, tourism or hospitality — a new category, built by a brand households already let into their kitchens every morning. Curation and trust are the product; accommodation is only one enabler inside it.
04 — Demand Signals

Eight forces feeding the Reconnection Economy.

These are behaviours documented in adjacent industries — travel, wellness, work — cited as evidence of a shift in what urban India wants, not as a description of Country Delight's own category.

Urban burnout

Rising density and always-on work culture are pushing short, frequent nature breaks over long annual holidays as the primary recovery pattern.

Experience-first thinking

Statista and IBEF both note a shift toward off-the-beaten-path destinations and cultural immersion over checklist sightseeing among Indian travellers.

Wellness-seeking

Adjacent wellness-travel research values India's market at roughly $29–35 billion depending on the firm, with digital-detox escapes the fastest-growing sub-category at a projected 17.3% CAGR (2026–2031) — a strong proxy for appetite for slow living.

Eco-consciousness

Government-backed river tourism, electric-solar boats and eco-circuits (Swadesh Darshan 2.0) signal state-level investment in sustainable, nature-linked infrastructure.

Staycations

Statista's domestic tourism outlook explicitly names the surging popularity of staycations, driven by changing preferences and flexible work options.

Work from anywhere

An estimated 60–90 million Indians (roughly 10–15% of the workforce) were projected to work remotely by 2025, creating a base of professionals who can extend a weekend from a laptop bag.

Sustainable living

Farm-based, produce-linked experiences align naturally with a dairy and fresh-food brand already positioned around sustainable sourcing.

Family bonding

Group and multi-generation travel remain a structural strength of Indian domestic travel behaviour, underpinning demand for farm and nature formats built for families rather than solo travellers.

09 — Competitor Landscape

Three categories of adjacent player. One clear gap.

Accommodation platforms solve finding a place. Experience platforms solve finding an activity. Nature experience brands come closest — but none combine trust, healthy living, food, nature and family into one ecosystem the way Country Delight can.

Accommodation Platforms — they solve finding a place to stay

Airbnb

Strength
Global inventory scale; established host trust systems and review infrastructure.
Weakness
Overwhelming choice with no India-specific curation for nature or farm formats.
Opportunity for CD
Win on curation depth where Airbnb wins on raw supply breadth.

Booking.com

Strength
Massive hotel and property inventory with strong conversion-optimised booking flow.
Weakness
Statista describes it as transaction-first, built around booking volume rather than experience discovery.
Opportunity for CD
Lead with "how do you want to feel," not a filter panel of room types.

MakeMyTrip

Strength
Deep India distribution; recently added AI-led trip planning via a partnership with OpenAI (announced Feb 2026).
Weakness
Built around flights and hotel transactions, not curated nature or wellness journeys.
Opportunity for CD
Own the "nature and wellness" niche MakeMyTrip's AI tools aren't designed around.

Airbnb Experiences

Weakness

Nature Experience Brands — they come closest, but neither is a full ecosystem

Weakness
Primarily a product (dairy) brand; farm visits are a secondary offering, not the core product.

Take the single-farm authenticity model and make it a multi-city, discoverable network.

Country Delight's position: none of the nine brands above combine trust, healthy living, food, nature, family and authenticity into a single curated ecosystem. That combination — not inventory size — is the actual competitive space.

13 — Product Opportunity

This is not a hospitality play.

What it looks like from the outside

Country Delight Stays will look, on the surface, like it competes with villa platforms and booking apps. It doesn't. It is building a curated ecosystem of trusted nature experiences, where accommodation is one component among several — alongside food, activities, and the people who host them.

The one sentence that matters

Accommodation is only one part of the experience. The experience is the product.

14 — Product Discovery Insight

The single biggest insight in this report.

Every accommodation and travel platform in this report starts with geography. Country Delight starts with intent — destination comes last, not first.

"Where do you want to go?"
"How do you want to feel?"
Step 1
How do you want to feel?

Recharge, reconnect, disconnect, or bond with family — the entry point is emotional, not geographic.

Step 2
What kind of experience?

Family Farm Day, Work from Nature, Digital Detox, Wellness Retreat, Slow Living — matched to the feeling chosen.

Step 3
How much time do you have?

A few hours, a full day, or a weekend — set expectations before location narrows the options.

Step 4
How far will you travel?

How far someone is willing to go, given the time available — destination enters the flow here, and only here.

Step 5
Recommended Experiences

A short, curated shortlist — not an endless, unranked list of listings.

15 — Conclusion

Problem, opportunity, differentiation, next steps.

Problem

Discovery for authentic nature experiences in India is fragmented, and trust — not supply — is the real constraint on adoption.

Opportunity

A large and accelerating category — India's agritourism market alone is forecast to grow at roughly 15% annually through 2030 — with no dominant, trusted curator yet.

Differentiation

An existing, earned relationship of trust with millions of urban households, extended from the breakfast table to the weekend getaway.

Next Steps

Start narrow: pilot day experiences in one or two metro-adjacent farm clusters, validate the "how do you want to feel" discovery model, then expand toward immersive, overnight formats.
